Repugnant Laws

Judicial Review of Acts of Congress from the Founding to the Present

A comprehensive historical and empirical study of the Supreme Court’s exercise of judicial review from the Founding to the present, challenging the narrative of a countermajoritarian Court by demonstrating how judicial power often aligns with political coalitions.
